Drug Classifications

Anabolic

Anabolic steroids had been initially synthesized in 1930 with therapeutic intentions in mind. In spite of popular controversy, a large portion of the non-medical steroid using demographic does not consist of athletes. Hypodermic needles have been the passage of use, as it may escalate the process. Certain adverse effects may include, face irritations (e.g acne, pimples, etc) leading to the growth of tumors inside the body and may contain the potential to clog arteries in the circulatory region of the body. Lastly, it may take a toll upon a user’s mental health. Little research is done regarding recreational steroid use, however, legal counterparts of AS (anabolic steroids) are in circulation with documented effects.

Antidepressant

Antidepressants are rather interesting, in the sense that it, like many substances are never a guarantee in achieving their advertised effects. Antidepressants however, are solely instructed as therapeutic tools with little to no recreational value. The latter posing quite an irony as Antidepressants abusers compose the third largest demographic of prescription drug emergency visits. General Antidepressants, as discussed earlier, are solely instructed to be used as tools of therapy in treating an array of psychiatric disorders. The most common psychiatric “disabilities” may include Obsessive-compulsive disorder, Generalized Anxiety disorder, and most forms of depression. The efficacy of treating anxiety disorders (GAD & OCD included) has been well-documented, mostly supporting the claim that Antidepressants have a tendency to be moderately more effective than their placebo counterparts. The efficacy of Antidepressants in treating depression has been rather, inconsistent. The largest study on its efficacy when it came to treating depression, dubbed the "The Sequenced Treatment Alternatives to Relieve Depression” reported that those with higher levels of depression are less likely to achieve remission with antidepressants (at least SSRIs). The study had used the HAM-D17 questionnaire to determine the severity of the illness and had results in accordance to so, and the QIDS-SR16 scale to determine the extent of remission. Both of which, have limitations that pertain to interpretation (of severity & remission). Furthermore, cases of relapse having been quite significantly high in those taking antidepressants. Antidepressant withdrawal (or antidepressant use in general, but most notably occurring with withdrawal symptoms) has also been shown to have adverse effects, going as far to dampen the mental state of the patients. Antidepressants Pharmacology, “Culture”, further precautions The workings of antidepressants (or any substance) are incredibly complex, and require a great deal of knowledge in organic chemistry, as well as neurology (specific to this field). Psychologists and scientists alike however, constantly ponder the complexity of depressive symptoms. In it’s very simplicity, antidepressants have three most common classifications, NRIs, SSRIs, SNRIs (keep in mind there are several other antidepressants with a different set of pharmacological mechanics). The three have nuances in their pharmacological workings, but to discuss them would be futile, as the intended response for each of them are relatively the same (similar). They act to prevent the reabsorption of certain neurotransmitters known to increase mood, be it serotonin or noradrenaline. The psychoactive effects of the NRI classification however, can be addressed. As stimulants like cocaine possesses NRI activity. The side effects of antidepressants vary, however a general list of side effects (according to the Mayo clinic) may include, “Drowsiness,Nausea, Dry mouth, Insomnia ,Diarrhea , Nervousness, agitation or restlessness.” It is also not recommended to take antidepressants with mood-altering substances. And unlike recreational substances, antidepressants do not have a “visible” culture.

Antipsychotic

Yet again does a disclaimer addressing the inadequately of analyzing the essence of a substance in detail appear. Antipsychotics have been an ongoing controversy since the dawn of time, when caveman (and Scottish economist) Adam Smith had been in dire need of antipsychotics in suppressing his autistic rants. Like Antidepressants, the antipsychotic controversy lies in the efficacy of the substance; whether it proves successful or not. The latter being quite important, especially when addressing a therapeutic substance. Why take substances if they don’t work? General With that having been said, antipsychotics have been manufactured for specific therapeutic use, though that statement might be controversial in itself. It had been designed to stabilize the mood of, typically, the mentally ill. Like most intended therapeutic substances, the efficacy of antipsychotics, or lack thereof, merit its fair share of controversy. Some antipsychotics include Risperidone, Olanzapine, and Quetiapine. Pharmacology For further depth about the pharmacology of antipsychotics, the circumstances of its origin must be understood. Antipsychotic use originated in the 1950s, of which the therapeutic utility of drugs had been in its relative infancy--it had been used in replacement of lobotomy (a controversial issue of its own) to suppress the psychotic states of mentally ill patients. With that having been acknowledged, most antipsychotics aim to do just that, suppress the states of mentally ill patients. Antipsychotics act as a D2 receptor antagonist, occupying a receptor associated with the dopamine neurotransmission as well as blocking dopamine reuptake. Psychosis induced mania has been hypothesized to indirectly correlate with dopamine, “Presynaptic striatal dopaminergic function can be measured using radiolabeled L-dopa, which is converted to dopamine and trapped in striatal dopamine nerve terminals ready for release. This provides an index of the synthesis and storage of dopamine in the presynaptic terminals of striatal dopaminergic neurons (see review by Moore et al15). Seven out of 9 studies in patients with schizophrenia using this technique have reported elevated presynaptic striatal dopamine synthesis capacity in schizophrenia” Oliver D. Howes, Shitij Kapur; The Dopamine Hypothesis of Schizophrenia: Version III—The Final Common Pathway, Schizophrenia Bulletin, Volume 35, Issue 3, 1 May 2009, Pages 549–562, https://doi.org/10.1093/schbul/sbp006 The chart depicts the main function of all antipsychotics. Atypical vs Typical All antipsychotics act to inhibit dopamine. Antipsychotics having been first synthesized in the 1950s, have been modified into different mechanisms of operation. Atypical antipsychotics, dubbed “second-generation antipsychotics” aim to mainly target other receptors. Second-generation antipsychotics, however, are relative in a sense of semantics. They can be used to reference antipsychotics with an additional additive(such as an affinity with 5ht receptors) , a reduction upon adverse effects, secondary use as antidepressants. Recreational Use, Side effects & Miscellaneous Antipsychotics, conventional or atypical, are not recommended for recreational use, as they provide little recreation value. In spite of this, recreational users of antipsychotics do quite in fact exist, high doses of brand name antipsychs can produce sedation. Recreational users capitalize on this sedation alone; doing so, however, would be akin to drinking vanilla extract for its relative high alcohol content. It’s worth noting that consumption of certain second-generation antipsychotics may suppress psychedelic exposure as they bind to similar receptors (5ht), though not all SGAs bind with similar receptors.

Cannabinoid

Cannabis is as specific type of a psychoactive drug. it slows down the messages traveling from the brain and throughout your body. And if taken with more than a normal dose, it can produce an hallucinogenic effect because of the most active chemical called THC (delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol). The most common use of this drug is by smoking ( presumably with a bong or marijuana cigarette) or adding a few drops on the tip of a cigarette. It may be mixed with food through baking cookies or brownies. Cannabis by far is the most popular substance (after alcohol that is), and as a result much documentation has been done.

Psychedelics

“Psychedelics are an overly complex classification of substances, that would result in endless discussion, and is ultimately pointless to discuss in brevity…” ~David Higgs Forward Psychedelics, as noted previously, are substances to be discussed thoroughly, and like the many other substances that have been discussed, will not have been analyzed in detail. Organic Chemists and Pharmacologists have written dissertations, in their attempt to fully analyze the pharmacology of psychedelics. Countless studies could amount to the sole understanding behind psychedelic culture (or rather counter-culture), the severe complexity of psychedelics could not be stressed enough. General There are several types of psychedelics, most of which, again, need to be discussed in great detail, as they have extreme differences. There’s a general consensus that psychedelic substances have little abuse potential. Overdose itself is nearly impossible, and physical dependence upon psychedelics are typically unheard of. Keep in mind, however, that any substance, chemical, habit can become addictive, though purely psychological. The power of the human psyche, especially when substances enter the question, should ,however, never be undermined. As, studies have even reported the very-real effects of placebos. Psychedelics in question, however, operate to a much higher potency than their placebo counter-parts. Psychedelics include, LSD, DMT, Mescaline, Psilocybin Mushrooms. Pharmacology Any study made upon the pharmacology of psychedelics would not come about, lacking a set of disclaimers regarding the limitations of said study. With that having been said, most psychedelics produce effects because of their relationship to monoamines, psychedelics having a relationship to, “serotonin (5-HT), adrenaline (epinephrine), norepinephrine, and dopamine (DA).” Psychedelics being closely structured to monoamines, bond to natural receptors found in the blood stream. LSD (a commonly used psychedelic) binds to most serotonin receptors, acting as a partial agonist; therefore, we can assume that LSD (specifically) has a higher structural affinity for those natural (monoamine) receptors in the bloodstream. The chart depicts the psychedelic molecules by order of 5ht1b affinity (which is the most common receptor, for psychedelic affinity). It’s suggested that substances such as MDMA, do not have affinity for 5ht receptors (being the main reason why it fails in belonging to the psychedelic class, yet reserves effects of psychedelics) and therefore cannot produce psychedelic effects to the same potency. Now these receptors are chiefly responsible for a variety of functions, of which are not limited to: motor coordination, fear, sense of survival, ego, logic, memory, etc. Typically monoamines are not capable of entering the bloodstream, however the specific charge of psychedelics (being neutral), allow them to bypass the bloodstream. The chart suggests that LSD has had more affinity with “5ht” receptors, than DMT has had; therefore explaining why LSD is more difficult to metabolize. It’s worth mentioning that psychedelic effects are as unpredictable as the 5ht receptors they bond with. Having a variety of 5ht receptors, it’s difficult to analyze the functions of specific receptors that the psychedelic molecules bond with. Side effects & Precautions *Please conduct further research before administering the usage of psychedelics The unpredictability of psychedelic side-effects, would only suggest the user conduct general safety measures. Certain safety measures might include, a sitter, testing your substance, proper setting. The extensive manufacturing process of Lysergic Acid Diethylamide (LSD), has been known to attract counterfeit substances. These substances can range from breath-mints to life-threatening drugs; the only way to ensure a safe trip is to test your substance using the marquis reagent. A simple drop, followed by a color change will insurance your substance, is as advertised. Certain side effects may include: “Physical Effects Dilated pupils Higher or lower body temperature Sweating or chills (“goosebumps”) Loss of appetite Sleeplessness Dry mouth Tremors Mental Effects Delusions Visual hallucinations An artificial sense of euphoria or certainty Distortion of one’s sense of time and identity Impaired depth perception Impaired time perception, distorted perception of the size and shape of objects, movements, color, sounds, touch and the user’s own body image Severe, terrifying thoughts and feelings Fear of losing control Panic attacks Flashbacks, or a recurrence of the LSD trip, often without warning long after taking LSD Severe depression or psychosis” Also, it’s worth noting that in accordance with long-term psychedelic use, the risk of mental illness is quite real and maybe life-threatening.

Sedative

Preface/Forward GHB & GBL A sedative, by definition, is any substance that is capable of inducing sedation. The latter establishes an overlap over the many substances that have previously discussed, however our primary objective is to discuss substances that capitalize off of inducing sedation. Pharmacology No substance solely capitalizes off of sedation, however, several benzodiazepines bind to the GABA-a1 and the GABA-a5 associated with sedation. Precautions Sedative consumption with the additive of alcohol may escalate sedation to dangerous magnitudes.

Depressant

Depressants-Sometimes called “downers,” these drugs come in multicolored tablets and capsules or in liquid form. Some drugs in this category, such as Zyprexa, Seroquel and Haldol, are known as “major tranquilizers” or “antipsychotics,” as they are supposed to reduce the symptoms of mental illness. Depressants such as Xanax, Klonopin, Halcion and Librium are often referred to as “benzos” (short for benzodiazepines). Other depressants, such as Amytal, Numbutal and Seconal, are classed as barbiturates—drugs that are used as sedatives and sleeping pills Short term effects of Depressants-Slow brain function, Slowed pulse and breathing, Lowered blood pressure, Poor concentration, Confusion, Fatigue, Dizziness, Slurred speech, Fever, Sluggishness, Visual disturbances, Dilated pupils, Disorientation, lack of coordination, Depression, Difficulty or inability to urinate,and Addiction Long term effects of Depressants-Long-term use of depressants can produce depression, chronic fatigue, breathing difficulties, sexual problems and sleep problems. As a dependency on the drug increases, cravings, anxiety or panic are common if the user is unable to get more. Higher doses can cause impairment of memory, judgment and coordination, irritability, paranoia, and suicidal thoughts. Some people experience the opposite of the intended effect, such as agitation or aggression. Using sedatives (drugs used to calm or soothe) and tranquilizers with other substances, particularly alcohol, can slow breathing and the heart rate and even lead to death Withdrawal symptoms include insomnia, weakness and nausea. For continual and high-dose users, agitation, high body temperature, delirium, hallucinations and convulsions can occur. Unlike withdrawal from most drugs, withdrawal from depressants can be life-threatening.

Benzodiazepine

Benzodiazepines, like most substances are synthesized with therapeutic intentions in mind, specifically to deter anxiety. The mechanism operates by reducing the activity of the brain and spinal cord, to enhance the effectivity of a neurotransmitter called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA).This particular neurotransmitter suppresses the excessive activity of the nerves, which has been in association with anxiety and mental instability.

Stimulants

Stimulant are used to increase alertness and energy. Common pills that falls into this category are cocaine and amphetamines. They can be swallowed, burned and injected, or crushed and inhaled. Effects may vary to a long term and a short term use . This can increase exhaustion and escalate the urge to take more. Since they are very addictive, it can cause paranoia,abnormal body temperature and heartbeats.

Opiates

Opiates, dubbed ‘narcotics’, can be naturalised drugs that are extracted & refined through the opium poppy plant. On the other hand, the term Opioid refers to a chemically synthesized version of the former, such as, morphine, codeine, oxycodone, and heroin. These drugs are created to treat severe pains and coughs, induce sleep, and give relief to the bowel. These drugs are highly addictive and if abused there are dangerous side effects. With the evident opioid crisis on the rise, and with no means of proper dissolution, we’re forced to turn to social stigmas and ostracisms as the means of avoidance. While being a relatively effective means of deterrence (in other issues), this may fail to apply to substance abuse. Many addicts will continue to abuse in spite of the ostracisms and risks involved. This may be the case, as many studies have shown a strong correlation with socioeconomic circumstances with opioid use. This is strongly believed that recreational opiate use should be taught (NOT encouraged) as it is very easy to overdose and/or sustaining a severe injury. Opiates like oxycodone are typically consumed in pill form, but can be ‘parachuted’ (or crushed then consumed/snorted), the effects are relatively quick to occur. The main safety concern pertains to the popularity of IVs(intravenous injection) in opiates consumption, also recognized as, ‘shooting up.’ In any case dosage and intake must be carefully measured.
